## Garden maintenance in Clontarf

Clontarf sits on one of the quietest stretches of Sydney Harbour — Middle Harbour, tucked behind Dobroyd Head, with the beach at one end, the reserve behind you and the spit bridge at your front door if you're heading anywhere. It's Northern Beaches but it doesn't feel like the surf beaches. The gardens here reflect that. They're sheltered, a bit humid, mostly on sloping blocks that drop away towards the water, and they grow fast in spring. If you stop looking for a month in November, by Christmas you're starting from behind.

Rob Gardening & Maintenance works Clontarf on the regular Northern Beaches rotation, and we know the streets well — the steep driveways off Sandy Bay Road, the long hedges along Monash Crescent, the little reserve-backed gardens where the bush line is doing half the landscaping for you. It's one of our favourite areas to work.

### What's different about Clontarf gardens

The first thing is the slope. A lot of Clontarf blocks are stepped — front lawn, side terraces, back lawn dropping towards the water. That means we're carrying kit up and down stairs, working around retaining walls, and planning the clean-up path before we even start. It's not hard, it's just something you have to be set up for.

The second thing is the bush. Clontarf butts right up against Clontarf Reserve and the bushland that runs across Dobroyd Head into Manly. A lot of back fences are reserve boundaries. That's great for privacy and for wildlife — but it also means native seedlings are constantly blowing in from next door, and if you want a clean lawn line you have to stay on top of it.

The plant palette is softer than Rose Bay — less salt, more humidity — so frangipani, gardenia, camellia, hibiscus and the big leafy tropicals all do well here. Lilly pilly, murraya and viburnum hedges are the most common street frontages. Kikuyu and couch lawns. Not much buffalo.

Wildlife is standard Northern Beaches harbourside — brushtail possums, rainbow lorikeets, bush turkeys if you're close to the reserve, and the occasional water dragon if you're near the gully. Bush turkeys will rearrange your mulch faster than you can put it down. That's a known thing.

### What Rob and the crew do in Clontarf

- **Regular garden maintenance** — the full rotation. Mowing, edging, hedge shape-ups, bed weeding, feeding, mulching, deadheading the flowering shrubs, tidying the reserve-edge bush line.
- **Hedge trimming and structural pruning** — lilly pilly, murraya, viburnum, photinia, camellia. Regular shape cuts in the growing season, one heavier reset a year to keep them dense at the base.
- **Lawn care** — small to medium kikuyu and couch lawns, including the stepped and terraced ones that need mowing the hard way. Edging, feeding, top-dressing if it's getting patchy.
- **Bush-boundary clean-ups** — tidying the fence line where the reserve meets your back yard, pulling native seedlings out of the lawn, keeping it neat without stripping the privacy.
- **Pre-sale garden makeovers** — Clontarf property changes hands at Sydney prices, and we do a lot of pre-sale reset work. One or two visits and the garden is camera-ready. Tell us the open date and we'll work back from it.
- **Pool surround maintenance** — most Clontarf back gardens have a pool, and the stuff that grows around them (palms, strelitzia, ferns) needs regular attention so it doesn't drop half its leaves in the water every week.
- **Green waste removal** — we take the prunings with us. No trailer loads sitting by your garage waiting on council cleanup.
