## Garden maintenance in Rose Bay

Rose Bay gardens look easy from the street and aren't, once you're inside the front gate. The blocks are narrow, the slopes drop hard towards the harbour, the mature palms are thirty years older than the current owner and the salt spray off the water is slowly pickling anything that isn't planted for it. The postcard view is doing a lot of work to distract you from how much ongoing attention a proper Rose Bay garden actually needs.

Rob Gardening & Maintenance works across Rose Bay on a regular rotation, and we know what the Eastern Suburbs throw at a garden that the Lower North Shore doesn't. It's a different plant palette, a different microclimate and a different style of work than Mosman. You have to respect what the salt and sun are going to do to a plant over a decade, not just this week.

### What's different about Rose Bay gardens

Rose Bay is narrow Eastern Suburbs terraces and waterfront homes, and the plants that survive long term are the ones that don't mind the salt — frangipani, bougainvillea, bird of paradise, agave, the tougher palms, and whatever native plantings the landscape architect chose for the front. Lawns are often small kikuyu patches rather than big runs, which is lucky because kikuyu is one of the few grasses that handles the Eastern Suburbs summer without sulking.

The slope is the other thing. A surprising number of Rose Bay gardens are actually three or four terraced levels pretending to be one back yard. Carrying prunings up to the kerb can be the longest part of the job. We plan for it.

Possums and lorikeets are the usual visitors — same as Mosman — plus the occasional cockatoo wandering in from the Centennial Parklands direction. Nothing unusual, but enough to keep the fruit trees honest.

And like Mosman, property turnover is high. A lot of what we do in Rose Bay is pre-sale — reset the garden, stage it for the open homes, make it look like someone has loved it for the last five years even if they've been renting it out and leaving it to the tenants.

### What Rob and the crew do in Rose Bay

- **Regular garden maintenance** — the full rotation. Mowing, edging, bed weeding, hedge shape-ups, deadheading, feeding, mulching. The whole cycle, on a schedule, so you never have to chase anyone.
- **Hedge trimming and pruning** — lilly pilly, murraya, photinia, bougainvillea (if you want to be brave), and the flowering shrubs that line most Rose Bay frontages.
- **Pre-sale garden makeovers** — reset the garden in one or two visits so the listing photos and the first open home look like the home has been properly cared for. If you're listing, tell us the open date and we'll work back from it.
- **Salt-tolerant replanting** — if you've lost something to the salt or the westerly, we'll tell you what's actually going to survive in its place and replant it. No point putting a camellia in a front garden that gets hit with the afternoon wind off the harbour.
- **Palm care** — regular dead-frond removal, trunk cleaning and feeding for the mature palms that define a lot of Rose Bay front yards.
- **Small-space courtyard work** — the tight terrace gardens where every plant is visible and every leaf counts. These need a lighter touch, not a big-kit approach.
- **Green waste removal** — we take everything with us. The kerb on bin night is not where your prunings live.
