## Garden maintenance & ride-on mowing in Terrey Hills

Terrey Hills is not like the rest of Sydney. Drive a few minutes off Mona Vale Road and the quarter-acre blocks stop and the one- and two-acre blocks start. Long gravel driveways. Horse paddocks. Bushland that runs straight up into Ku-ring-gai Chase. You can see the city from some of the ridges and still hear nothing but cockatoos. It's the kind of place where a push mower gives up by the time you hit the halfway point of the front lawn.

That's where we come in. Rob Gardening & Maintenance services Terrey Hills properties on our regular Northern Beaches rotation. Most of the work up here is ride-on territory — half-acre lawns through to genuine acreage frontages — and we bring the right kit for it. The ride-on does the big open ground, the hand tools come out for the garden beds, the pool surrounds, and the bits close to the house where it actually has to look sharp.

### What's different about gardens up here

Terrey Hills gardens have a few quiet problems you don't get in the harbourside suburbs. Grass in a wet October will get away from you fast, and grass getting away from you isn't just ugly up here — it's a genuine fire risk. Most properties sit inside a bushfire-prone designation and the RFS Asset Protection Zone guidelines are worth taking seriously. Keeping the 10 to 20 metres around the house short, clean and clear isn't a nice-to-have; it's the difference between "a good summer" and a very bad one.

Native plantings dominate most front yards — banksias, grevilleas, hakeas, tea trees — and they want different treatment to the hedges and camellias you'd find in Mosman. Lantana creeps back into cleared margins every year. Possums help themselves to the citrus. Cockatoos go at anything that catches their eye. The kookaburras are the only ones that won't cost you money.
